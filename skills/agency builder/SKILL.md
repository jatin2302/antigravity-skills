# Digital Marketing Agency Builder

A comprehensive skill for building, analyzing, managing, and continuously improving digital marketing agencies.

## Core Capabilities

This skill handles the complete lifecycle of digital marketing agency management:

1. **Analysis & Assessment** - Deep dive into existing agencies or market opportunities
2. **Strategic Planning** - Business positioning, service offerings, and growth strategy
3. **Operational Excellence** - Workflows, processes, and systems
4. **Client Management** - Acquisition, delivery, and retention systems
5. **Team Structure** - Roles, hiring, and organizational design
6. **Sales & Marketing** - Agency promotion and lead generation
7. **Financial Planning** - Pricing, projections, and profitability
8. **Continuous Monitoring** - Status checks and ongoing optimization

## When to Use This Skill

Trigger this skill when the user wants to:
- Start a new digital marketing agency
- Analyze and improve an existing agency
- Create agency workflows and standard operating procedures
- Develop service packages and pricing models
- Build client acquisition or delivery systems
- Optimize agency operations
- Get strategic guidance on agency growth
- Monitor agency health and performance
- Create agency templates and documentation

## Approach

### Phase 1: Discovery & Analysis

**For Existing Agencies:**
1. Conduct comprehensive agency audit across all dimensions
2. Identify strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats
3. Benchmark against industry standards
4. Assess current workflows, team, clients, and finances
5. Document pain points and bottlenecks

**For New Agencies:**
1. Understand founder's background, skills, and resources
2. Analyze market opportunity and competition
3. Define target niche and ideal client profile
4. Assess available budget and timeline
5. Identify gaps and required capabilities

**Questions to Ask:**
- What's your current situation? (existing agency or starting from scratch)
- If existing: What services do you offer? How many clients? What's working/not working?
- If new: What's your background? What niche are you targeting?
- What are your goals? (revenue targets, team size, lifestyle)
- What's your timeline and available resources?
- What specific challenges are you facing?

### Phase 2: Strategic Foundation

Create or refine:

**Business Strategy:**
- Agency positioning and unique value proposition
- Target market definition and ideal client profile
- Competitive differentiation strategy
- Service portfolio and package structure
- Pricing strategy and profit margins
- Growth roadmap with milestones

**Service Offerings:**
- Core service packages (tiered offerings)
- Service delivery process for each package
- Deliverables and timelines
- Resource requirements per service
- Upsell and cross-sell opportunities

### Phase 3: Operational Infrastructure

Build comprehensive operational systems:

**Workflows & Processes:**
1. **Client Acquisition Workflow**
   - Lead generation process
   - Qualification criteria
   - Sales process and scripts
   - Proposal templates
   - Contract and onboarding

2. **Service Delivery Workflow**
   - Project kickoff process
   - Execution steps by service type
   - Quality control checkpoints
   - Client communication cadence
   - Revision and approval processes

3. **Client Success Workflow**
   - Ongoing account management
   - Performance reporting
   - Quarterly business reviews
   - Retention and renewal process
   - Referral request system

4. **Internal Operations Workflow**
   - Team communication standards
   - Project management system
   - Time tracking and productivity
   - Knowledge management
   - Tool stack and integrations

**Templates to Create:**
- Sales scripts and email sequences
- Proposal template
- Contract/MSA template
- Client onboarding checklist
- Project brief template
- Creative brief template
- Reporting templates
- Invoice templates
- SOPs for each service type

### Phase 4: Team & Organizational Design

**Structure:**
- Organizational chart for current and future state
- Role definitions with responsibilities
- Hiring roadmap and priority positions
- Contractor vs. employee decisions
- Compensation structure

**Key Roles to Define:**
- Founder/CEO responsibilities
- Account managers
- Project managers
- Specialists (by service type: SEO, PPC, content, social, etc.)
- Creative roles (designers, copywriters)
- Support roles (admin, finance)

### Phase 5: Sales & Marketing System

**Agency Marketing:**
- Brand positioning and messaging
- Website strategy and content
- Lead generation channels
- Content marketing plan
- Social media strategy
- Case study development
- Referral program

**Sales Process:**
- Lead qualification criteria
- Sales pipeline stages
- Discovery call framework
- Proposal development process
- Objection handling
- Closing techniques
- Follow-up sequences

### Phase 6: Financial Models

**Pricing Models:**
- Cost-plus pricing calculations
- Value-based pricing frameworks
- Retainer structures
- Project-based pricing
- Performance-based models
- Package comparisons

**Financial Planning:**
- Revenue projections (12-month, 3-year)
- Expense budgets by category
- Cash flow projections
- Profitability analysis
- Break-even analysis
- Growth investment plan

**KPIs to Track:**
- Monthly recurring revenue (MRR)
- Client acquisition cost (CAC)
- Customer lifetime value (LTV)
- Gross margin by service
- Team utilization rates
- Client churn rate
- Net promoter score (NPS)

### Phase 7: Implementation & Monitoring

**Implementation Plan:**
- Prioritized action items
- Quick wins vs. long-term initiatives
- 30-60-90 day roadmap
- Responsible parties for each item
- Success metrics

**Ongoing Monitoring:**
Create a health check system that evaluates:
1. **Financial Health**
   - Revenue trends
   - Profit margins
   - Cash flow status
   - Client concentration risk

2. **Operational Health**
   - Project delivery timelines
   - Client satisfaction scores
   - Team capacity utilization
   - Process adherence

3. **Growth Health**
   - Pipeline size and quality
   - Conversion rates
   - Client retention rate
   - Service mix evolution

4. **Team Health**
   - Employee satisfaction
   - Turnover rate
   - Skill gaps
   - Workload balance

## Continuous Improvement Approach

When monitoring or checking in on an agency:

1. **Status Check:**
   - Review KPIs against targets
   - Identify variances and trends
   - Flag areas of concern
   - Highlight wins and successes

2. **Root Cause Analysis:**
   - Dig into underperforming areas
   - Interview/survey stakeholders if needed
   - Review processes and systems
   - Identify bottlenecks

3. **Optimization Recommendations:**
   - Prioritized improvement opportunities
   - Specific action items with owners
   - Expected impact quantification
   - Implementation difficulty assessment

4. **Iterative Refinement:**
   - Test and measure improvements
   - Document learnings
   - Update SOPs and templates
   - Scale what works

## Best Practices

**Be Practical:**
- Start with minimum viable processes, then iterate
- Focus on high-impact, low-effort wins first
- Don't over-engineer - simplicity scales better
- Use proven templates and frameworks

**Be Specific:**
- Include actual numbers in financial models
- Create detailed step-by-step workflows
- Provide real examples in templates
- Give concrete metrics to track

**Be Comprehensive:**
- Address all aspects of the business
- Think through edge cases
- Consider scalability from the start
- Plan for both success and setbacks

**Be Actionable:**
- Clear next steps with owners
- Realistic timelines
- Measurable outcomes
- Built-in accountability

## Key Questions Throughout

**Strategy:**
- Who is the ideal client and why would they choose this agency?
- What makes this agency different from competitors?
- What's the path to $1M, $5M, $10M in revenue?

**Operations:**
- Can this process be documented and delegated?
- What would break if we 10x'd the business?
- How do we maintain quality at scale?

**Team:**
- What roles are critical vs. nice-to-have?
- Build, buy, or partner for this capability?
- How do we retain top talent?

**Finance:**
- Are we pricing for profitability or just revenue?
- What's our cash runway and burn rate?
- Which services drive the most profit?

**Growth:**
- What's the most efficient client acquisition channel?
- How do we increase client lifetime value?
- When should we expand services vs. go deeper in our niche?

The skill is successful when it produces:
1. Clear, actionable strategic direction
2. Documented workflows that can be followed by team members
3. Financial models with realistic projections
4. Templates that save time and ensure consistency
5. Ongoing recommendations that drive measurable improvements
6. A framework for continuous optimization

## Notes

- Always adapt recommendations to the agency's specific context, resources, and goals
- Balance ambition with realism based on available resources
- Consider the founder's strengths and build around them
- Remember that people and culture matter more than processes
- Focus on sustainable growth, not just rapid expansion
- Profitability beats revenue growth
- Client success is the foundation of agency success
